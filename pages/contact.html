Bio

A native of Indianapolis, I grew up playing ball (there’s only one ball in Indiana, and it’s round, orange and dimpled all over) and drawing comic book characters with friends, but I was most interested in product packaging. To me, the art on the box wrapped around a Super Mario 3 cartridge was as awesome as the game itself. I funneled that passion for design into my high school newspaper when my dreams of NBA stardom were crushed after being cut from the basketball team (I still love hoops, though). With that opportunity, I developed a knack for visual storytelling that has carried me to where I am today.

After learning how to be a journalist at my college paper, the Indiana Daily Student, and a short stint at the Tucson Citizen, I joined the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette in 2005 as a designer for the sports section of the newspaper. I quickly learned to trust my instincts while creating dynamic presentations on tight, nightly deadlines. I tried to channel the energy and intensity that I observed in sports into the headlines, graphics and charts in the paper.

Next, I moved down the hall to the Post-Gazette art department where I was fortunate enough to learn from talented illustrators and graphic designers that helped shape my design identity. I absorbed concepts on color, spatial relationships, typography and balance that sharpened my eye while designing and art-directing many of the paper’s biggest projects — print and digital.

With those design tools in hand, I jumped feet-first into web design where I continue to expand my ability to code. By absorbing the fundamentals of html, css and javaScript, I am able to bring my designs to life across all platforms and devices.

Today, I’m the digital design editor at the PG — a job that allows me to lead a team of talented writers, photographers and developers to produce insightful and engaging online experiences.

I’m proud to have earned a few attaboys throughout my career, including 15 Awards of Excellence from the Society of News Design (with three individual portfolio awards) and honors from the Associated Press Sports Editors, Pennsylvania News Media Association, Pittsburgh Black Media Federation, Society of Professional Journalists, and the Press Club of Western Pennsylvania.
